  • Anachie Gordon
    Anachie Gordon is a traditional Scottish ballad, also known as Lord Saltoun and Auchanachie. The ballad's origins are somewhat obscure, but it dates back to the 18th or early 19th century. The song tells the tragic story of a young woman, Jeannie, who is in love with Anachie Gordon. Despite her love for Anachie, she is forced to marry another man, Lord Saltoun. She dies of a broken heart shortly after, and Anachie arrives too late to save her. The setting is likely in Scotland, reflecting the themes of love, loss, and the harsh consequences of forced marriage.
  • Auld Lang Syne
    Auld Lang Syne is a traditional Scottish song written by the poet Robert Burns in 1788. It is set to the tune of a traditional folk melody. The phrase auld lang syne translates to old long since or days gone by, and the song is a nostalgic reflection on friendship and the passage of time. It is famously sung to bid farewell to the old year and welcome the new one, particularly at the stroke of midnight on New Year's Eve. The song is also used at various other farewell occasions and is celebrated for its theme of cherishing old acquaintances and memories. Auld Lang Syne has become an integral part of New Year traditions around the world.
  • Awa' wi' Your Witchcraft o' Beauty's Alarms
    Awa' wi' Your Witchcraft o' Beauty's Alarms is a lesser-known Scottish song written by the renowned poet Robert Burns. The song is a humorous rejection of superficial beauty and charms in favor of a deeper, more genuine attraction. Burns, who lived from 1759 to 1796, wrote many such works that delve into human emotions and societal observations with his characteristic wit and insight. The song, like many of Burns' compositions, reflects themes of love, beauty, and sincerity, often using Scots dialect and traditional melodies. It is part of Burns' extensive body of work that continues to be celebrated in Scotland and around the world.
  • Believe Me If All Those Endearing Young Charms
    Believe Me If All Those Endearing Young Charms is a song written by the Irish composer Thomas Moore in 1808. It’s a sentimental piece reflecting on the enduring beauty of youth and love. Notable for its lyrical expression and melodic charm, it remains a classic in Irish music.
  • Beware o' Bonnie Ann
    Beware o' Bonnie Ann is another charming song penned by Robert Burns. The song is a playful warning about the allure of a beautiful woman named Ann. Through witty lyrics and a catchy melody, Burns captures the light-hearted side of romance and the caution that often accompanies it.
